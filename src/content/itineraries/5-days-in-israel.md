Only have five days? This itinerary captures the essence of Israel — its sacred heart, its desert drama and its Mediterranean cool — without feeling rushed. It assumes you fly into and out of **Ben Gurion Airport (TLV)**, sleep three nights in Jerusalem and two in Tel Aviv, and move by train plus one guided day trip. No rental car needed. Got longer? See the [7-day](/itineraries/7-days-in-israel) and [10-day](/itineraries/10-days-in-israel) versions, or focus the whole trip on the capital with [3 days in Jerusalem](/itineraries/3-days-in-jerusalem). Just here for the city? The [2 days in Tel Aviv](/itineraries/2-days-in-tel-aviv) itinerary covers the beach, Jaffa and the neighbourhoods as a standalone trip.

The pacing below assumes a Sunday-to-Thursday run, which keeps you clear of Shabbat closures (Friday afternoon to Saturday evening). If your five days span a Friday–Saturday, do the Dead Sea day on Saturday — the desert sites run normally and shared *sherut* taxis cover the transport gap — and eat in the Christian Quarter or East Jerusalem where restaurants stay open.

## Before You Go: The Practical Frame

**How to get from the airport.** The **Yitzhak Navon high-speed train** runs from Ben Gurion straight to Jerusalem in about 30 minutes for **₪18 (about $5)**, roughly every 30 minutes Sunday–Thursday and Friday morning; it stops for Shabbat. Tap in with a **Rav-Kav** card (₪5 to buy, top up at any station machine) or pay contactless with the Hopon/Moovit app. A *sherut* shared taxi or private cab runs ₪250–320 ($70–90) and is the only option during Shabbat.

**Where to stay.** Three nights in **Jerusalem** around **Mamilla or the city centre** puts you walking distance from both the Old City and the nightlife; two nights in **Tel Aviv** near the beach or **Rothschild/Florentin** for the food and bars. Expect ₪450–800 ($130–220) a night for a solid mid-range hotel in either city, or ₪110–160 ($30–45) for a hostel dorm. Book ahead — rates spike hard around Passover, the High Holidays, Christmas and Easter.

**Money, dress and insurance.** ATMs are everywhere and cards are accepted almost universally; carry ₪150–200 cash for market stalls and *sherut* taxis. Cover shoulders and knees at every religious site (men get a paper kippah at the Western Wall; women want a scarf for churches and the plaza). Carry [travel insurance](/israel-travel-insurance) for any Israel trip — hospital costs are reasonable but not trivial.

## Day 1 — Arrive & Jerusalem's Old City

**Morning / midday.** Land, clear the airport, and take the train into [Jerusalem](/jerusalem) (30 min, ₪18). Drop bags near Mamilla and grab a falafel or sabich lunch (₪25–35) before you start walking.

**Afternoon.** Walk into the [Old City](/jerusalem/old-city) through Jaffa Gate. Make for the [Western Wall](/jerusalem/western-wall) plaza first — open 24 hours, free, the afternoon light on the Herodian stones is excellent. Then thread five minutes north to the [Church of the Holy Sepulchre](/jerusalem/holy-sepulchre) (open from 04:00; free) and do a first loop of the four quarters. Allow three to four hours and don't try to "finish" the Old City today — you're orienting.

**Evening.** Dinner at the [Mahane Yehuda market](/jerusalem/mahane-yehuda), ten minutes from the centre on foot or one light-rail stop (₪6). From Thursday night the daytime stalls flip into the city's best bar street; a sit-down dinner at Machneyuda or a graze through the alleys runs ₪90–160 ($25–45).

**Stay:** Jerusalem (night 1 of 3). **Day spend, mid-range:** ~$50–80 plus your hotel.

## Day 2 — Jerusalem in Depth

**Morning.** Start early. If your dates align, the **[Temple Mount / Haram al-Sharif](/jerusalem/temple-mount)** non-Muslim entry windows are typically Sunday–Thursday, 07:30–10:30 and 12:30–13:30; entry to the platform is free but the Dome of the Rock and Al-Aqsa interiors are for Muslim worshippers only. Enter via the Mughrabi Bridge from the Western Wall plaza, expect a security line, and leave bags light. Then trace the [Via Dolorosa](/jerusalem/via-dolorosa) from the Lion's Gate through the Stations of the Cross back toward the Holy Sepulchre.

**Afternoon.** Choose one anchor: [Yad Vashem](/jerusalem/yad-vashem), Israel's Holocaust memorial (free, closed Saturdays, allow three hours, under-10s not admitted to the main museum), or the [Israel Museum](/jerusalem/israel-museum) for the Dead Sea Scrolls and the Second Temple model (around ₪64 / $18, allow two hours). Yad Vashem is the more affecting; the Israel Museum is better in heat or rain.

A **guided Old City walk** pays for itself today — the Status-Quo politics, the Roman Cardo and the rooftop viewpoints are hard to read solo. Half-day group walks run about **$40–55**; book the day before.

**Evening.** A second, slower evening in Mahane Yehuda, or head to **Emek Refaim** in the leafy German Colony (Caffit, Tmol Shilshom) — a 12-minute light-rail ride.

**Stay:** Jerusalem (night 2 of 3). **Day spend, mid-range:** ~$70–110.

## Day 3 — Masada & the Dead Sea

The classic Jerusalem day trip, and the single best use of a guided tour on this itinerary because public transport to Masada at dawn is impractical.

**Pre-dawn / morning.** Pickup around 03:30–04:00 for the **sunrise climb of [Masada](/dead-sea/masada)** — the Snake Path takes about an hour each way, or the cable car runs from 08:00 (park entry ₪31 / $9; cable car adds ₪48 / $14 each way). Sunrise over the Moab mountains from Herod's fortress is the photograph of the trip.

**Late morning.** Descend to the [Ein Gedi](/dead-sea/ein-gedi) nature reserve (₪29 / $8) for a short waterfall hike through the oasis, then a **float in the [Dead Sea](/dead-sea)** — the lowest point on Earth at 430m below sea level. Smear on the mineral mud, float (don't swim — keep the water out of your eyes), and rinse thoroughly at the free showers. Bring sandals; the shoreline pebbles and salt crystals are sharp.

**Booking & cost.** Guided Masada–Ein Gedi–Dead Sea day trips from Jerusalem run about **$95–120** including transport and a guide (park fees sometimes extra); they return you to the city by mid-to-late afternoon. Book through the affiliate tour cards below or a [Dead Sea day-tour search](/dead-sea).

**Evening.** Back in Jerusalem, an easy dinner near your hotel — you'll be tired and salty.

**Stay:** Jerusalem (night 3 of 3). **Day spend, mid-range:** ~$110–140 (tour-driven).

## Day 4 — Transfer to Tel Aviv & Old Jaffa

**Morning.** Check out and take the train from Jerusalem–Yitzhak Navon to [Tel Aviv](/tel-aviv) (about 35–45 minutes, ₪18–25). Drop bags at your beach-area or Florentin hotel.

**Afternoon.** Walk ancient [Old Jaffa](/tel-aviv/old-jaffa) — the flea market, the restored stone lanes, the harbour, and the Old Jaffa lookout for the skyline panorama. Stroll back north along the **Tayelet** beach promenade ([Tel Aviv beachfront](/tel-aviv/tayelet)); rent a Tel-O-Fun share bike (₪17/day access) if you'd rather ride. A first dip in the Mediterranean is free and excellent.

**Evening.** Dinner in [Neve Tzedek](/tel-aviv/neve-tzedek) (pretty, pricier) or buzzing [Florentin](/tel-aviv/florentin) (street art, cheap eats, late bars). A casual dinner runs ₪70–130 ($20–37); cocktails ₪48–60.

**Stay:** Tel Aviv (night 1 of 2). **Day spend, mid-range:** ~$60–100.

## Day 5 — Tel Aviv & Departure

**Morning.** A morning of [Bauhaus White City](/tel-aviv/rothschild) architecture — the UNESCO-listed cluster around Rothschild Boulevard, best self-guided with a free map or a Bauhaus Center walk — and the [Carmel Market](/tel-aviv/carmel-market) for a final graze. Or skip both and just take the beach.

**Midday.** Train from Tel Aviv (HaShalom or Savidor Center) back to **Ben Gurion** — only 20–25 minutes, ₪18. Allow a generous buffer: Israeli airport security wants you there **three hours** before an international departure.

**Stay:** Departure. **Day spend, mid-range:** ~$30–50 plus airfare.
