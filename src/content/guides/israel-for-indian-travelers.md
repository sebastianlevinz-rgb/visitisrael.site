Israel receives a growing number of Indian visitors — drawn by Christian pilgrimage routes, Jewish heritage connections, the Dead Sea, and a fascination with one of the world's most ancient and complex countries. Since July 2025, the Indian eVisa-B2 process has made the trip significantly easier to plan. This guide covers the essential practical information for Indian passport holders: the visa process, flights, vegetarian food, connectivity and safety.

## The most important thing first: you need an eVisa

This is the single most critical piece of planning for Indian passport holders. **India is NOT on Israel's ETA-IL visa-exempt list.** The ETA-IL (Israel's electronic travel authorisation, similar to the US ESTA) is only available to citizens of a specific set of countries — India is not among them.

**Indian citizens must apply for a B/2 Tourist eVisa before boarding any flight to Israel.** There is no visa on arrival.

### The eVisa-B2 online application (since July 2025)

The Israeli Embassy in New Delhi launched an online eVisa-B2 system for Indian passport holders on **1 July 2025**. This replaced the previous appointment-based sticker visa process. You can now apply online from anywhere in India:

- **Apply at:** embassies.gov.il/india (official Israeli Embassy India portal)
- **Fee:** ₪50 (approximately ₹1,600–1,700 at recent exchange rates; fee is charged in shekels)
- **Processing time:** minimum 3 weeks — apply at least **4–5 weeks before travel**
- **Validity:** typically up to 90 days, with the actual period of stay determined at the port of entry
- **Approval:** The Israeli Embassy reports a high approval rate for tourism applications with complete documentation; approval is not guaranteed

### What documents you will need

- Passport valid for at least 6 months beyond your intended stay in Israel
- Recent passport-size photographs meeting Israeli embassy specifications
- Confirmed return flight itinerary (booking confirmation is sufficient; a ticket purchase is not required)
- Hotel reservation confirmation (a Booking.com confirmation letter is accepted as proof of accommodation)
- Bank statements for the last 3–6 months showing sufficient funds
- Travel insurance policy covering the full duration of your trip
- Any previous Israel visas or travel history (if applicable)

**Do not apply through unofficial third-party websites** — they often charge significantly more than the official ₪50 fee and are not affiliated with the Israeli government. The official portal is embassies.gov.il/india.

## Flying from India to Israel

### El Al direct service: Delhi (DEL) to Tel Aviv (TLV)

El Al Israel Airlines operates a **non-stop direct service** between Indira Gandhi International Airport (DEL) and Ben Gurion Airport (TLV). This is the fastest and most convenient option:

- **Flight time:** approximately 7 hours westbound; slightly longer eastbound
- **Frequency:** several times weekly (check elal.com for current schedule — frequencies vary seasonally)
- **Jet lag:** minimal. Israel Standard Time is UTC+3 in summer and UTC+2 in winter; IST (Indian Standard Time) is UTC+5:30. The time difference is just 2.5–3.5 hours — far less adjustment than flights to Europe or North America

### Connecting flights via Gulf hubs

If El Al's direct route is full or not price-competitive for your travel dates, connecting options via Gulf hubs are efficient:

| Hub | Carriers | Total journey time |
|---|---|---|
| Dubai (DXB) | Air India, IndiGo, Flydubai + Emirates | 8–10 hours |
| Doha (DOH) | IndiGo + Qatar Airways | 8–10 hours |
| Abu Dhabi (AUH) | Air India + Etihad | 8–11 hours |
| Istanbul (IST) | Turkish Airlines | 9–12 hours from major Indian cities |

There are no non-stop services from Mumbai, Bangalore, Chennai, Kolkata or Hyderabad at time of writing — travellers from these cities connect via Delhi or a Gulf hub.

**When to book.** Fares from India to Israel are lowest in January–February and October (outside Israel's peak seasons). The most expensive periods are Passover (March–April), the Jewish High Holidays (September–October), the summer peak (July–August) and the Christmas–New Year window. See the [cheap flights guide](/cheap-flights-to-israel) for airline comparison tools.

## Christian pilgrimage for Indian travellers

The Indian Christian community — particularly from Kerala, Tamil Nadu, Goa and Nagaland — is among the fastest-growing pilgrim segments visiting Israel. Israel contains the most significant sites in Christian tradition, concentrated in a compact geography:

### Key pilgrimage sites

**Galilee region:**
- **Nazareth** — the Basilica of the Annunciation (largest church in the Middle East; over the site where the angel Gabriel is said to have appeared to Mary); Mary's Well; the Synagogue Church
- **Sea of Galilee** — the Church of the Beatitudes (Sermon on the Mount); Tabgha (Multiplication of the Loaves and Fishes); Capernaum (Jesus' base of ministry; ruins of the synagogue he is said to have preached in)
- **Yardenit** — the Jordan River baptism renewal site at the southern end of the Sea of Galilee; popular with Indian pilgrims for baptism or renewal of vows; clean, well-managed, free entry

**Jerusalem:**
- **Via Dolorosa** — the route Jesus walked to the crucifixion; 14 Stations of the Cross through the Muslim Quarter and Old City
- **Church of the Holy Sepulchre** — the site of the crucifixion, burial and resurrection according to Christian tradition; deeply moving for pilgrims; no entry fee; arrive early (before 8am) to avoid heavy crowds
- **Garden of Gethsemane** — the olive grove where Jesus prayed before his arrest; centuries-old olive trees; Church of All Nations adjacent

**Bethlehem (West Bank):**
- **Church of the Nativity** — birthplace of Jesus; UNESCO World Heritage Site; located in Bethlehem in the Palestinian Authority (not Israel proper). Most pilgrimage tours include Bethlehem as a day trip from Jerusalem; the checkpoint crossing is straightforward for international tourists with valid passports. See the [Jerusalem–Bethlehem day trip guide](/jerusalem-bethlehem-day-trip) for logistics.

### Pilgrimage tour options

Organised day tours from Jerusalem or Tel Aviv cover multiple pilgrimage sites efficiently. GetYourGuide and Viator offer both group and private options with English-speaking licensed guides. Indian Christian associations and churches sometimes organise dedicated group pilgrimages — check with your denomination or diocese for group tour programmes, as these often include Indian pastoral leadership during the tour.

## Vegetarian and dietary considerations

Israeli cuisine is naturally well-suited to vegetarian travellers — the country's food culture centres on vegetables, legumes, dairy and eggs.

### What Indian vegetarians can eat freely

- **Hummus** — the national staple; made from chickpeas, tahini, lemon and garlic; dairy-free and vegan
- **Falafel** — deep-fried chickpea or broad-bean balls; universally available; vegan
- **Shakshuka** — eggs poached in spiced tomato sauce; vegetarian; widely served for breakfast and lunch
- **Sabich** — pitta sandwich with fried aubergine, hard-boiled egg, hummus and Israeli salad; vegetarian
- **Mezze spreads** — baba ganoush, tabbouleh, labneh, Israeli salad, stuffed vine leaves (some contain meat — check)
- **Israeli salad** — finely diced cucumber, tomato and onion; always vegan
- **Dairy (chalavi) kosher restaurants** — these restaurants serve no meat at all under kosher law; they are entirely safe for lacto-vegetarians

### Kosher and vegetarian overlap

Israel's kosher restaurant system creates a practical advantage for vegetarians: restaurants certified as **dairy-kosher** (chalavi) serve no meat and are a de facto vegetarian restaurant category. Look for the kosher certificate (teudat kashrut) displayed at the entrance. Vegetarian-friendly chains include **Café Landwer** (dairy kosher, extensive salad and eggs menu, widespread across Israel) and numerous neighbourhood dairy cafés.

### Tel Aviv's vegan scene

Tel Aviv has one of the world's highest concentrations of vegan restaurants per capita. Recommended options:

- **Meshek Barzilay** — organic farm-to-table; south Tel Aviv; popular with local health-conscious crowd
- **Anastasia** — all-day brunch café; large vegan selection; popular near Florentin
- **Buddha Burgers** — plant-based burgers; multiple Tel Aviv branches
- **Abu Hassan (Jaffa)** — the most famous hummus restaurant in Israel; dairy-free and vegan; arrive early as queues form quickly

### Jain dietary requirements

Strict Jain dietary practice (no root vegetables, no onion/garlic) is not available in dedicated Jain restaurants in Israel — the food culture does not have a category that maps to this. Self-catering is easiest: Israeli supermarkets (Shufersal, Rami Levy, Victory) have excellent fresh produce sections. The Carmel Market in Tel Aviv and Mahane Yehuda Market in Jerusalem are outstanding for fresh vegetables, legumes and grains. Bring your own spice mix if you follow a strict regimen.

### Halal food

Halal-certified restaurants are available in East Jerusalem (Muslim Quarter), Arab neighbourhoods (Akko, Nazareth, Jaffa, Haifa's Wadi Nisnas) and Israeli-Arab towns. Wolt and Google Maps "halal" filters are increasingly reliable in these areas. **Kosher ≠ halal** — pork is forbidden under both systems, but the animal slaughter methods and certification authorities differ; Muslim travellers who require halal certification should verify with the specific restaurant.

## Mobile phone and connectivity

### Indian SIM roaming in Israel

All three major Indian carriers (Jio, Airtel, Vi/Vodafone Idea) offer international roaming packs for Israel. Rates and data allowances vary by plan — compare current options on each carrier's website before travel, and activate the pack before departure.

### Better options for data

1. **Israeli tourist SIM** — available at Ben Gurion Airport arrivals hall (Golan Telecom and Hot Mobile kiosks, typically open around the clock). A 30-day unlimited-data SIM card costs approximately ₪50–90 (~₹1,250–2,250) and includes calls within Israel. Your Indian phone must be unlocked to use a local SIM; most current Jio, Airtel and Vi handsets are network-locked — check with your carrier before travel.

2. **eSIM** — providers such as Airalo and Nomad offer Israel data eSIMs that you activate on your phone before leaving India. Compatible with iPhone XS and later, most Samsung Galaxy S20+ series and later, and other eSIM-capable handsets. No physical SIM swap needed — useful for keeping your Indian number active for two-factor authentication while also having Israeli data.

### Payments: UPI is NOT accepted

**UPI (Unified Payments Interface), BHIM and PhonePe are not accepted in Israel.** Israel operates on standard international card infrastructure. What works:

- **Visa and Mastercard** — accepted universally across Israel, including contactless (tap-to-pay)
- **Rupay** — limited acceptance; Rupay Global (via Discover/Diners network) works at some Israeli terminals but is not as reliable as Visa/Mastercard
- **Google Pay / Apple Pay** — work in Israel if linked to a Visa or Mastercard
- **UPI / BHIM / PhonePe** — not accepted

**Before you travel:** contact your Indian bank to confirm that international transactions are enabled on your card and that the spending limit covers your trip. Some Indian banks require a call or SMS activation for overseas use. Axis Bank, HDFC, ICICI, SBI and Kotak all issue Visa/Mastercard debit cards with international functionality; inform your bank of your travel dates to prevent security blocks.

**Cash.** Carry some New Israeli Shekel (NIS/₪) cash — approximately ₪200–400 — for markets, small cafés, shared taxis (sheruts) and rural areas. Airport exchange desks offer poor rates; withdraw NIS from Bank Leumi or Bank Hapoalim ATMs in the city instead. Avoid dynamic currency conversion (DCC) at ATMs — always choose to be charged in NIS.

## Time zone and jet lag

Israel Standard Time is **UTC+3 in summer** (last Sunday of March to last Sunday of October) and **UTC+2 in winter**.

India is UTC+5:30 year-round.

| Season | Israel time | IST | Difference |
|---|---|---|---|
| Summer (late March – late October) | UTC+3 | UTC+5:30 | India is 2h 30min ahead |
| Winter (late October – late March) | UTC+2 | UTC+5:30 | India is 3h 30min ahead |

**Jet lag is minimal** — the time shift is only 2.5–3.5 hours, far less than a trip to Europe (5.5h behind) or North America (10.5h+ behind). Most Indian travellers adjust within a day. On the El Al direct flight departing Delhi in the evening (typical schedule), you arrive Tel Aviv the same evening local time — a good window to have dinner and go to bed at a normal hour.

## Power adapter

Israel uses **Type H sockets** — a unique three-flat-pin format (V-shape or Y-shape) used almost nowhere else in the world. Indian plugs (Type D — the round three-pin format used in India and much of South Asia, or Type C — the two-round-pin European format used on some Indian electronics) do not fit without an adapter.

**Voltage is compatible.** Israel runs on 230V/50Hz — the same voltage range as India (220–240V/50Hz). Your Indian phone charger, laptop adapter and camera charger will work in Israel with just a **plug adapter** (no voltage converter needed, as long as the device is marked "100–240V" — nearly all modern electronics are).

Buy a Type D/C to Type H adapter before you leave India — they are inexpensive on Amazon India (search "India to Israel travel adapter") and considerably cheaper than options at Ben Gurion Airport.

## Planning your Israel trip from India

A 10-day itinerary covers Israel comfortably for most Indian travellers: **2–3 nights Tel Aviv** (beaches, White City architecture, Carmel Market, vibrant food scene); **3–4 nights Jerusalem** (Old City, Western Wall, Church of the Holy Sepulchre, Yad Vashem); a **Galilee day trip or overnight** (Nazareth, Sea of Galilee, Yardenit, Tabgha — especially significant for Christian pilgrims); and a **Dead Sea half-day** (Ein Gedi or Kalia Beach). The [5 vs 7 vs 10 days guide](/israel-5-vs-7-vs-10-days) helps calibrate the right trip length.

For the Galilee and Negev, a **rental car** makes the most sense — roads are excellent, English signage is clear, and India's right-hand traffic is the same side as Israel. Your Indian driving licence must be accompanied by an **International Driving Permit (IDP)** in Israel — the IDP is issued in India by the Automobile Association of India (AAI) and regional transport authorities. See the [Israel driving guide](/driving-in-israel) and the [car hire guide](/car-rental-israel) for details.
