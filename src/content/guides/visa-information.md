For most Western travellers, entering Israel as a tourist is straightforward — but since 2025 there's one extra step to do **before you fly**: the ETA-IL travel authorization. This is general guidance; **always confirm the rules for your nationality with an official Israeli government source** before booking, as requirements can change.

## Visa-free entry

Citizens of the **United States, United Kingdom, the EU/Schengen area, Canada, Australia, New Zealand** and many other countries can visit Israel **visa-free for tourism for up to 90 days** — but visa-free no longer means paperwork-free. Travellers from these visa-exempt countries now need an **ETA-IL** (below) before travelling. Citizens of countries that are *not* visa-exempt must instead arrange a **B/2 visitor visa** at an Israeli embassy or consulate in advance.

## The ETA-IL (electronic travel authorization)

Since **1 January 2025**, most visa-exempt travellers must obtain an **ETA-IL** — an Electronic Travel Authorization — *before boarding* a flight to Israel. It's a quick online pre-screening, **not a visa**, and applies to citizens of the visa-exempt countries above (the US, UK, EU/Schengen, Canada, Australia and many others). **Israeli citizens and holders of an Israeli ID number don't need one.**

What to know:

- **Where to apply:** only through the official government portal, [israel-entry.piba.gov.il](https://israel-entry.piba.gov.il/). Apply directly — third-party sites add a markup for the same authorization.
- **Cost:** a government fee of **₪25 (about $7)**, paid by credit card. The online form takes roughly 10–15 minutes.
- **When to apply:** at least **72 hours before** your flight. Most approvals are near-instant, but some take up to 72 hours — don't leave it to the airport.
- **Validity:** once approved, it's generally good for **multiple trips for up to two years** (or until your passport expires, whichever comes first), each stay up to **90 days**.
- **What it does — and doesn't:** an approved ETA-IL lets you *travel to* the border. As with any country, the **border officer makes the final decision** on entry.

Fees, eligible countries and rules can change — always confirm the current details on the [official ETA-IL portal](https://israel-entry.piba.gov.il/) before you travel.

## The paper entry slip

Israel typically **does not stamp passports** at Ben Gurion Airport. Instead you're given a small **paper entry slip** at passport control. Keep it safe — hotels may ask for it at check-in, and you may need it at internal checkpoints. The slip also keeps any Israel entry record out of your passport, which can simplify later travel to certain countries.

## At the border

Expect security questions on arrival — about your trip, where you're staying and whom you know in the country. This is routine; answer honestly and have your accommodation details handy. Your ETA-IL approval and a passport valid for at least six months are the documents to have ready. Carry your passport throughout your trip, as you'll show ID at attractions and transport hubs.

## Extending your stay & overland entry

If you need longer than your permitted stay, you can apply for an extension at a Population and Immigration Authority (Misrad HaPnim) office before your current authorization expires.
