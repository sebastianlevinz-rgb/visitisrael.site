Israel is better known for its archaeology and beaches than its stadiums — but for the sports-minded traveller, the country offers genuine bucket-list live experiences: a Maccabi Tel Aviv EuroLeague night in one of Europe's loudest arenas, a marathon through the streets of the Old City, and the quadrennial Maccabiah Games. This guide covers the key events, how to get tickets, and how to plan a sports-focused trip to Israel.

---

## Maccabi Tel Aviv basketball

### The most storied basketball club in Europe

**Maccabi Tel Aviv** is the dominant force in Israeli sport and one of the most decorated clubs in European basketball history. With six EuroLeague titles (the most of any non-NBA team), 56 Israeli Premier League titles, and a fanbase that fills the Menora Mivtachim Arena on most nights, a Maccabi game is one of the loudest live-sports experiences available in the Middle East.

The **EuroLeague season** runs October through May, with the regular season giving way to the Top 16 and Final Four (held in a different European city each year). Home games in the regular EuroLeague season against clubs like Real Madrid, Panathinaikos, or CSKA Moscow regularly draw near-capacity crowds with a high proportion of international visitors in the stands.

### Attending a game

**Venue:** Menora Mivtachim Arena (Yad Eliyahu Arena), Yad Eliyahu, south Tel Aviv. Capacity: approximately 11,700.

**Tickets:** Buy through the official Maccabi TLV website (maccabi.co.il) or from secondary ticket platforms. EuroLeague nights sell faster than Israeli Premier League games — aim for 1–4 weeks ahead depending on the opponent. Prices typically run ₪100–₪300 for standard seats; courtside and VIP sections cost more.

**Getting there:** Bus lines 46, 61, or 68 from central Tel Aviv; or taxi/Gett (₪25–40 from central TLV). The Tel Aviv Light Rail provides useful connections. On match nights the stadium is easy to navigate — follow the crowd.

**Atmosphere:** Expect yellow and blue flags, drum sections, deafening noise on big European nights, and a stadium food experience that includes falafel, bourekas, and Israeli street food alongside the usual arena snacks. Fans mix Hebrew chants with crowd-standard European basketball atmosphere. English is widely spoken around the arena.

## Jerusalem Marathon

### The world's most dramatic urban marathon

The **Jerusalem Marathon** (typically held in early March) is internationally regarded as one of the most visually spectacular and physically demanding urban marathon routes anywhere. The full 42.2 km course crosses the Old City by the Western Wall observation point, winds through East Jerusalem and over the Mount of Olives, passes the Knesset, and descends through residential Jerusalem neighbourhoods — a combination of religious significance and serious elevation change that makes Jerusalem a genuine distance-running pilgrimage.

**Distances:** Full marathon, half marathon (21 km), 10 km, and family run. Tourists participate heavily in the 10 km and half marathon categories; the full marathon draws serious runners who train specifically for the hills.

**What makes it distinct:** Running through the gate of the Old City, past 2,000-year-old stone walls and through the neighbourhood streets of West Jerusalem, with the entire city out on the pavements cheering, is an experience available nowhere else on earth.

**Entry and planning:** Registration opens approximately 9–12 months before race day at the official Jerusalem Marathon website. Book accommodation in Jerusalem or nearby at the same time — the race weekend fills the city, particularly hotels in the centre, German Colony, and Mamilla. A useful pairing: run the half marathon on Saturday morning and spend Sunday exploring the Old City without race-day crowds.

## Tel Aviv Marathon

### The big flat: fast urban racing on the Mediterranean coast

The **Samsung Tel Aviv Marathon** (typically late February or early March) is the largest road race in Israel, drawing 40,000+ participants from 60+ countries in recent years. The course is flat, fast, and scenic: it follows the seafront promenade, winds through the White City (UNESCO Bauhaus district), passes through the Yarkon Park, and finishes near the beach.

For competitive runners seeking a personal best, Tel Aviv's flat course and mild late-winter temperatures (15–22°C typical race morning) make it one of the better marathon options in the region. For tourist-runners, the beachfront sections and the White City passage combine training and sightseeing in a way that few city marathons manage.

**Distances:** Full marathon, half marathon, 10 km, family fun run.

**Entry:** Via telavivmarathon.com; registration typically opens 9–12 months before race day. Book accommodation near the seafront (Hayarkon Street, the beach hotels along the Tel Aviv Promenade) for the shortest walk to the finish area.

## Israeli football (soccer)

### Premier League action in Tel Aviv and Jerusalem

**Israeli Premier League football (Ligat HaAl)** runs from mid-August through May with a winter break in January. For football tourists, there are three meaningful match-going options:

**Maccabi Tel Aviv FC** — the country's most successful football club (26 Premier League titles) plays at **Bloomfield Stadium** in south Tel Aviv, capacity ~16,000. The club also competes in UEFA club competitions (typically the UEFA Europa League or Conference League). International UEFA nights at Bloomfield generate genuine atmosphere. Local derbies against Hapoel Tel Aviv (at Bloomfield, or away at Bloomfield when Hapoel plays there under ground-share arrangements) are the most intense fixtures of the domestic season.

**Beitar Jerusalem FC** — Jerusalem's most supported club plays at **Teddy Kollek Stadium** (commonly called "Teddy") in west Jerusalem, capacity ~21,600. A Beitar home game — particularly against Maccabi Tel Aviv — gives visitors an experience of Jerusalem sports culture that is quite distinct from the pilgrimage trail: noisy, passionate, and very Israeli.

**Hapoel Tel Aviv FC** — plays at Bloomfield Stadium; the working-class counterpart to Maccabi TLV in the city's football identity.

Fixture lists and tickets are available at ligat-haal.co.il. Prices typically run ₪60–₪150. On most weekdays there is no need to book in advance; for derbies and end-of-season title races, buy ahead.

## The Maccabiah Games

### The Jewish Olympics — quadrennial, next in 2030

The **Maccabiah Games** are held every four years in Israel and constitute the world's third-largest multi-sport games by athlete count. The 22nd Maccabiah Games ran 30 June–13 July 2026, with athletes from 90+ countries competing across 42 sports at venues throughout Israel — Bloomfield Stadium, the Wingate Institute (Netanya), Jerusalem's Teddy Kollek Stadium, and specialised venues for swimming, tennis, and equestrian events.

For tourists visiting Israel during a Maccabiah year, the games offer access to a wide range of sports across 14 days at low or no cost. Many sessions at smaller venues are free; opening and closing ceremonies at Bloomfield require tickets in advance.

The **next Maccabiah Games are scheduled for 2030** — the 23rd edition. Check maccabiah.com for future dates and registration information as an athlete or spectator.

For full spectator logistics for the 2026 games or future editions, see the dedicated [Maccabiah Games spectator guide](/maccabiah-games-2026).

## Other annual sports events

### Tel Aviv International Tennis (Israel Open)

The **Israel Open** is an ATP 250-level tournament typically held in Tel Aviv or Ra'anana in February. ATP 250 events feature players ranked inside the world top 100; ticket prices are significantly lower than the equivalent Grand Slam or ATP 500 events, and the relaxed atmosphere makes it a good spectator experience. Current year fixtures and tickets via the Association of Tennis Professionals (atptour.com) or Israel Tennis Association (ita.co.il).

### Jerusalem-Tel Aviv road cycling

Israel hosts road cycling events through the season, including stages of the Giro Cycling Classic Israel. The country's compact geography (Galilee to Eilat in five hours by car) makes it possible to attend multiple stages of a multi-day race without complex logistics.

### Krav Maga and martial arts events

Tel Aviv and Jerusalem regularly host international martial arts competitions — BJJ, judo, and Krav Maga demonstrations and tournaments. These are usually smaller-venue events with open spectator access. The Israel Martial Arts Sports Association maintains a national events calendar.

## Planning a sports trip to Israel

**Best time for spectator sports:** October through May covers both the full EuroLeague basketball season and the Israel Premier League football season. The Tel Aviv and Jerusalem marathons fall in late February–early March, making that a particularly sport-dense window.

**Jerusalem or Tel Aviv as base?** Tel Aviv is the practical sports base — Maccabi TLV basketball and football are based there, and the Tel Aviv Marathon starts and finishes by the seafront. For the Jerusalem Marathon, stay in Jerusalem for race weekend and consider the city itself as part of the running experience. The two cities are 70 km apart and connected by express trains (35 minutes) and frequent Egged buses — day-tripping between them for events is entirely practical.

**Shabbat:** Israeli football and basketball do not schedule matches on Friday evening (from roughly sunset) through Saturday night, in keeping with Shabbat. Sunday is a standard match day in Israel. Jerusalem Marathon race day is typically Saturday morning, which is significant for observant Jewish runners — but the race organisers accommodate the timing within Israeli law (the race starts before full Shabbat closure would apply in practical terms). Always confirm current rules and race-morning logistics directly with event organisers.

**Ticket safety:** Avoid purchasing from unofficial scalpers on the street. Official team websites and reputable secondary-market platforms are the safest options.
