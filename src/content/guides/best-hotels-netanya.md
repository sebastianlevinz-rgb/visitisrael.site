**Netanya** is the most underrated beach hotel base in Israel. It sits on a limestone cliff above a 14-kilometre Mediterranean shoreline midway between Tel Aviv and Haifa — 45 minutes by train from Ben Gurion Airport, meaningfully cheaper than equivalent Tel Aviv accommodation, and with a cliff-top promenade that Tel Aviv's flat Tayelet beach strip does not replicate.

The city has a large French-speaking Jewish community (approximately 60,000 francophones) and attracts substantial European charter tourism, particularly from France and the UK. For visitors whose priority is a beach holiday with straightforward access to the rest of the country, Netanya's hotel market offers real value at every tier.

For the full picture of Netanya — the cliff promenade, Independence Square, HaPeled Street, the Stern Diamond Factory, and day-trip connections — see the [Netanya travel guide](/netanya-guide). This page focuses on where to stay.

## Where to stay in Netanya: areas at a glance

**Cliff-top / Independence Square zone** is the heart of Netanya's tourist accommodation. Hotels here sit on or just back from the cliff edge, with sea views north and south along the coast. You are walking distance from the cliff elevators to the beach, the HaPeled Street café strip, and the promenade. The atmosphere is genuinely Mediterranean-resort: outdoor café chairs on the square, sea breeze, and a relaxed pace. This is the most convenient base for beach-focused visitors.

**Beach access zone** (hotels with direct cliff paths or close to elevator points) is a subset of the cliff-top tier for visitors who want to minimise the walk to the sand. Netanya's beaches — Sironit, Poleg — are Blue Flag maintained; the elevation change from cliff to sand is around 20–30 metres, handled by free public elevators or steps.

**City centre / inland** (5–15 minutes from the cliff) offers the best value in Netanya. The trade-off is losing the sea view; the practical benefits are proximity to everyday amenities (supermarkets, pharmacies, train station bus connection) and lower prices.

**Leonardo Club zone** sits in the mid-northern beach area. The Leonardo Club Netanya is the only large all-inclusive property in the city — popular with European charter groups, families, and visitors who want a single inclusive package price.

## Budget stays (₪400–650/night)

Several guesthouses and apartment-style properties in the city centre and southern areas of Netanya offer the lowest price point in the market. These tend to be smaller, family-run properties without sea views — practical bases for visitors combining Netanya with day trips to Caesarea, Haifa, and beyond.

Budget prices in Netanya have historically been more stable than in Jerusalem or Tel Aviv. There is less of an extreme Israeli holiday premium because the hotel market is sized for European charter volumes rather than domestic pilgrimage surges. French school holidays (February, April, July–August) do bring price increases at cliff-top properties.

₪400–650 covers basic private rooms at smaller guesthouses and simple apartment lets near the city centre and train station area.

## Mid-range hotels (₪650–1,100/night)

The mid-range tier in Netanya offers solid value relative to comparable Israeli city accommodation. Several three-star and boutique-adjacent properties sit on or near the cliff, delivering sea views or easy promenade access at prices well below the Tel Aviv equivalent.

**Ramada Hotel Netanya** is a reliable mid-range option: a familiar international brand with consistent standards, a pool, and a location that makes the cliff promenade accessible. A good choice for visitors who want predictable facilities at a reasonable price.

**Leonardo Club Netanya** operates an all-inclusive format and typically prices around ₪700–1,200 depending on package. Popular with European charter groups, especially French tour operators. The all-inclusive format removes daily meal decisions — useful for families. Verify what is included (meals, drinks, activities) at time of booking as packages vary between seasons.

₪650–1,100 covers most mid-range Netanya properties. Cliff-top zone options command a small premium over inland properties for the view and promenade access.

## Boutique and upper-end stays (₪1,200+/night)

**Seasons Hotel Netanya** is the best-positioned upper-end property in the city: a cliff-top hotel with panoramic sea views, a pool, and a location directly above the promenade. It is the closest Netanya equivalent to a boutique hotel with genuine design investment — notably more personal than the large resort chains. Book via Booking.com; advance booking is recommended for summer weekends and French holiday periods when it fills quickly.

**David Tower Hotel** is a larger cliff-top property with pool and full-service facilities, positioned near the promenade. It caters to international tourists and Israeli domestic visitors seeking a proper sea-view weekend stay.

₪1,200+ covers both properties in normal seasons. Prices rise considerably during July–August and French school holidays.

## Booking context and price patterns

Netanya's hotel market is driven by European charter volumes and Israeli domestic tourism. This dual pattern means the city does not have the extreme Passover or Jewish New Year surges of Jerusalem, but does have distinct July–August and French school holiday peaks.

**When prices rise**: July–August is the peak for both Israeli families and French charter visitors; Passover and Sukkot bring moderate Israeli domestic demand; the French February and April school holidays add European family demand in those windows. Outside these periods, Netanya is reliably affordable — often the best-value sea-view accommodation in central Israel.

**Shabbat note**: Netanya has a significant observant Jewish population. On Shabbat, many independent restaurants and shops near the cliff close. Hotels that cater to European charter groups handle Shabbat meals internally; if staying independently, identify your Friday evening and Saturday dining plan before arrival. The beach is accessible on Shabbat and the promenade is active with families on walks.

**Seasons Hotel booking**: Fills on summer weekends — book 4–6 weeks in advance for Friday or Saturday nights in July and August, and for the peak French vacation period.
