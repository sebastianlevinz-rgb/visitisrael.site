The Negev covers more than half of Israel's land area, and most visitors experience it through a car window. A multi-day hiking circuit changes that: on foot, the desert reveals its scale and geological strangeness in a way that a day trip cannot. The Makhtesh Ramon crater floor, the white chalk canyon of Ein Avdat, and the ridge walks above Sde Boker are experiences that belong to hikers, not drivers.

This guide covers a 3–5 day self-planned desert hiking circuit based around the central Negev, with day-by-day planning, water logistics, and the safety information that makes the difference between a remarkable experience and a dangerous one.

**A frank warning before you read further.** Negev multi-day hiking is genuinely intermediate to advanced terrain, not a beginner walk. The combination of extreme heat (May–September), remote sections with no water for 20+ km, limited mobile coverage in parts of the Makhtesh, and sparse emergency services demands proper planning. The itinerary below is for November–April only. If you are not confident in your desert navigation and water management, a guided trek is the right choice.

## Before You Go: Essential Logistics

### Getting There — A Car Is Essential

There is no public transport to most Negev trailheads. You need a hire car for all of the following access points: the Makhtesh Ramon crater floor (southern rim road), the Ein Avdat lower entrance, Avdat Nabataean ruins, and most overnight camping points outside Mitzpe Ramon town. [Compare car hire prices from all major operators](/car-rental-israel) — pick up in Tel Aviv, Jerusalem, Beer-Sheva or Ben Gurion Airport.

If you prefer to avoid the car logistics, guided multi-day Negev treks handle trailhead transport and water resupply.

### Mitzpe Ramon as Base

Mitzpe Ramon is the only practical town base for a multi-day Negev hiking circuit. It sits on the crater rim at 900m elevation — cooler than the crater floor and central to all the routes described below. Accommodation ranges from Beresheet Hotel (luxury, crater-rim position) to Selina Ramon (eco-glamping tents, mid-range) to a handful of guesthouses in town. Book well in advance for spring weekends, school holidays, and the Sukkot festival week — the town is small and fills quickly.

Mitzpe Ramon has a supermarket, several restaurants, a petrol station, and an INPA ranger office at the Ilan Ramon Space Center. Fill up on water, food, and information before any crater-floor excursion.

### Water — The Critical Variable

| Route section | Water availability |
|---|---|
| Mitzpe Ramon town | Tap water available; fill 5L+ before crater entry |
| Makhtesh crater floor | **Zero natural water** — carry all you need |
| Ein Avdat canyon | Spring at lower canyon (seasonal — check INPA); none elsewhere |
| Sde Boker area | Kibbutz facilities + INPA site water points |
| Avdat ruins | INPA site — water point at entrance |

For all crater floor days: carry **minimum 5 litres per person**, set out by 07:00, and be back in shade by 10:00 when temperatures forecast above 28°C. This is not cautious advice; it is the minimum.

### Gear Checklist

- Water: 5L+ capacity (mix of bottles and hydration bladder)
- Sun protection: wide-brim hat, SPF 50+, long-sleeve UV shirt
- Navigation: Amud Anan app (download offline maps), AllTrails, plus a physical map
- Emergency: personal locator beacon (PLB) or GPS messenger for remote sections; fully charged power bank
- Footwear: trail runners or light hiking boots with ankle support; gaiters for sand sections
- Night gear: the desert is cold at night (0–12°C in winter) even when daytime temperatures are pleasant

## The 3–5 Day Itinerary

The circuit below uses Mitzpe Ramon as a base for Days 1–3, then extends south or north for Days 4–5 depending on your interest and fitness.

### Day 1 — Makhtesh Ramon: Crater Rim Orientation

**Starting point:** Mitzpe Ramon town (arrive by early afternoon)
**Distance/effort:** Easy — rim promenade walk, 3–4 km, no significant elevation

Arrive in Mitzpe Ramon with enough time to set up accommodation and acclimatise before the more demanding days ahead. This first afternoon is about understanding the geological context before you enter the crater.

**Ilan Ramon Space Center.** The Space Center on the crater rim has an exhibition explaining the makhtesh geology. *Makhtesh Ramon is not a meteor impact crater and not a volcano.* It is a geological formation found only in the Negev and Sinai — produced by tectonic uplift and millions of years of water erosion hollowing out a natural dome. The crater is 40 km long, 2–10 km wide, and approximately 500 m deep. The Space Center also commemorates Israeli astronaut Ilan Ramon (who died in the Columbia disaster, 2003) — the town is named after the crater, not the astronaut. INPA pass valid here.

**Rim promenade.** The paved promenade west of the Space Center follows the crater rim with multiple viewpoints. Walk it in both directions before sunset — the scale of the crater registers differently in morning light versus the golden hour before dusk.

**Yotam Road sunset.** Drive or walk west along Yotam Road for the best crater sunset viewpoints. The multi-coloured volcanic outcrops on the crater rim turn dramatic shades of red and orange in evening light.

**Evening.** Dinner in Mitzpe Ramon (book ahead). Check the forecast for tomorrow — any temperature above 28°C at crater-floor elevation means an earlier start.

### Day 2 — Wadi Ardon: Full Crater Floor Day

**Starting point:** Mitzpe Ramon town southern edge
**Distance/effort:** 10–12 km, significant elevation change, rocky terrain — the hardest day
**Departure time:** 07:00 maximum; earlier in warm weather

The crater floor is the centrepiece of a Negev hiking circuit. Wadi Ardon descends from the town's southern rim into the crater via a trail through geological formations that compress hundreds of millions of years into a few kilometres of walking.

**Trail description.** The descent from the crater rim passes the **Prism viewpoint** — a layer-cake of multicoloured volcanic rock where geological strata are legible at eye level. Once on the crater floor, the trail follows Nahal Ardon's dry riverbed through limestone, granite, sandstone, and basalt. The **Carpenter's Workshop**, approximately 2 km into the descent, is the most-photographed feature: a cluster of basalt columns that fractured into near-hexagonal shapes during ancient volcanic cooling. Nubian ibex — the sure-footed desert mountain goat — move across the crater walls. In March–April, desert wildflowers cover sections of the floor: anemones, desert tulips, and small iris species.

**Water and safety on the crater floor.** There is no shade, no water, and no mobile signal in parts of the Makhtesh. Carry the full 5 litres. Download the AllTrails or Amud Anan GPS track before descending — the crater is featureless in sections and the trail junction at the crater floor is easy to miss. Tell the INPA ranger at the Space Center your planned route before descending.

**Return.** The trail can be walked as a loop returning to the northern crater rim, adding approximately 2 hours. Alternatively, arrange a car drop at the southern trailhead exit and drive back to town — the road to the southern access point runs inside the crater and offers different geological views.

**Afternoon.** Rest in Mitzpe Ramon. If you have energy: the **Alpaca Farm** (Ha-Meshek Be-Midbar), 5 minutes west of town, is a working farm with alpacas and llamas on the crater-edge plateau — short trekking routes along the rim, views, minimal sun exposure. A low-effort afternoon counterpoint to the morning's demands.

### Day 3 — Ein Avdat Canyon & Sde Boker

**Starting point:** Ein Avdat National Park lower entrance (30 min drive north of Mitzpe Ramon)
**Distance/effort:** 3–4 km, one-way canyon traverse with metal ladders, moderate
**INPA pass valid**

Ein Avdat is a spring-fed canyon through white chalk cliffs in the northern Negev — one of the most beautiful landscapes in Israel. The spring at the canyon's centre feeds a clear pool beneath a small waterfall. Euphrates poplars and Sodom's apple vegetation grow in the otherwise bare limestone gorge.

**Access.** The park has two entry points: the **lower entrance** (Route 40 near Kibbutz Sde Boker) and the **upper entrance** (further north). The standard traverse visits the lower canyon first, ascending via metal ladders and a narrow cliff path to the upper entrance. This direction gives the best visual experience — the spring and pool appear after a 30-minute walk through the narrowing canyon. Arrange a car shuttle between entrances, or walk back the way you came.

**Timing.** Arrive at opening (typically 08:00). The spring pools and ladder sections are confined — shade disappears quickly and by 10:00 in warmer months the lower canyon is hot. In November–February, the waterfall runs more substantially; in late summer the flow is minimal. Check current conditions via [parks.org.il](https://www.parks.org.il) before visiting.

**Wildlife.** Nubian ibex live permanently in the canyon cliffs and appear at the spring. Egyptian vultures and Griffon vultures circle overhead from the canyon rim. The spring pool has Marsh frogs.

**Afternoon — Sde Boker & Ben-Gurion's Grave.** [Kibbutz Sde Boker](https://www.ben-gurion-heritage.org.il) is 8 km north of Ein Avdat on Route 40. David Ben-Gurion chose to retire and be buried here — in the Negev, not in Jerusalem — as a deliberate statement about Israel's future. His simple wooden hut is preserved exactly as it was at his death in 1973. The grave overlooks the Zin Valley, a sweeping panorama of Negev Highlands that extends to Ein Avdat's cliffs. Entry is free. Allow 45–60 minutes.

### Day 4 (Extension) — Avdat Nabataean Ruins & Highlands Ridge

**Starting point:** Avdat National Park (20 km south of Sde Boker on Route 40)
**Distance/effort:** 2–3 km walking at site, minimal elevation
**INPA pass valid**

[Avdat](/negev-incense-route) is the best-preserved Nabataean-Byzantine city on the Negev Incense Route — a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The Nabataeans built this complex in the first century BCE as a caravanserai on the spice and incense trade route from Arabia to the Mediterranean. The remains include a winery complex, bathhouse, temple, residential quarter, and a Byzantine church built over the Nabataean structures. INPA rangers lead guided tours at fixed times.

A short trail from the Avdat site connects to a ridge walk with views across the surrounding highlands — combine with a late-afternoon return to Mitzpe Ramon.

**Optional: Negev Highlands wine tasting.** Several small wineries operate within 20–30 minutes of Mitzpe Ramon — Boker Valley Winery, Carmey Avdat, and Matar among them. The volcanic soil and high altitude produce wines distinct from the Mediterranean coast. Most require advance booking.

### Day 5 (Extension) — Southern Makhtesh Routes or Timna Park

**Option A — Southern crater floor access.** The southern access road into Makhtesh Ramon (via Negev Route 171 south) reaches sections of the crater not accessible from the Mitzpe Ramon town approach — the multicoloured sand canyon near Nahal Ardon's lower section, the remote basalt formations in the western end. These sections are reachable only by 4x4 or licensed jeep tour. Half-day and full-day jeep tours depart from Mitzpe Ramon — see the [Mitzpe Ramon guide](/mitzpe-ramon-guide) for current operators.

**Option B — Timna Park.** [Timna](/timna-park-guide) is 25 km north of Eilat — ancient copper mines, the mushroom rock, King Solomon's Pillars, and a reconstruction of the Tabernacle. Note: Timna is **not** covered by the standard INPA parks pass. It works better as a day trip from Eilat than from Mitzpe Ramon given the 140 km distance.

## Camping in the Negev

INPA operates designated campsites at several points in the central Negev. Wild camping is prohibited inside nature reserves and national parks.

| Campsite | Location | Booking |
|---|---|---|
| Makhtesh Ramon campsite | Crater rim, Mitzpe Ramon | parks.org.il — advance booking required |
| Ein Avdat campsite | Near lower entrance | parks.org.il — advance booking required |
| Sde Boker field school | Kibbutz Sde Boker | fieldschool.co.il or direct booking |

Campsites fill quickly during spring (March–May) and the Sukkot holiday week. Book weeks in advance. Each INPA campsite has basic facilities: picnic tables, fire pits (fuel prohibited — use designated grills only), toilet blocks, and water. No hot showers. Cold Negev nights require a 3-season sleeping bag even in March.

## Safety Summary

| Condition | Action |
|---|---|
| Temperature forecast above 28°C | Start crater hikes by 06:30, exit by 09:30 |
| May–September | Do not attempt multi-day desert hiking |
| Solo hiking | Leave route details + return time with someone; carry PLB |
| No mobile signal | Download offline GPS before entering crater; Amud Anan app |
| Flash flood warning in any wadi | Exit the wadi immediately and climb to high ground |
| Water uncertain | Do not proceed — water logistics cannot be improvised |

Emergency number in Israel: **101** (Magen David Adom / emergency services). Israel Trail Rescue: notify via Magen David Adom. INPA ranger contact at Makhtesh Ramon: [Ilan Ramon Space Center](https://mard.org.il).
