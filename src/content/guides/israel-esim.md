Staying online in Israel is easy and cheap if you sort it before you fly. An **eSIM** — a digital SIM you install with a QR code — is the simplest option for most visitors: buy a plan at home, land at **Ben Gurion Airport**, and you are connected within minutes, with no kiosk queue and no passport paperwork.

## Why an eSIM for Israel

Israel has excellent mobile coverage — three strong networks (Partner, Pelephone, Hot Mobile) with widespread 4G/LTE and growing 5G in [Tel Aviv](/tel-aviv) and [Jerusalem](/jerusalem). A travel eSIM rides those networks, so you get fast data the moment you arrive. Compared with the alternatives:

- **vs. carrier roaming** — usually far cheaper, with no bill-shock.
- **vs. a local SIM** — no passport registration, no shop visit, no swapping out your home SIM (you keep your number active for calls and texts).

## How to set it up

1. **Buy before you fly**, while you have Wi-Fi at home.
2. **Install the eSIM** by scanning the QR code (Settings → Add eSIM / Add mobile plan).
3. **On landing**, enable data roaming for the eSIM line — you connect automatically.

Most travellers do fine on a few gigabytes for a one-to-two-week trip; pick a larger or unlimited plan if you tether or stream.

## How much data do you need?

Match the plan to your trip length and how heavily you use data. These are rough guides — data amounts and prices vary by provider, so check the live price before you buy.

| Trip & usage | Suggested data | Rough price |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Light** — maps, messaging, a few days | ~1–3 GB | from ~$5 |
| **Standard** — maps, social, light browsing, ~1 week | ~5 GB | ~$8–15 |
| **Heavy** — streaming, tethering, 1–2 weeks | ~10 GB+ or unlimited | ~$20–35 |

A few tips that keep costs down:

- **Most sightseeing is light on data** — offline maps and messaging use very little; you rarely need unlimited for a city break.
- **Top up rather than over-buy** — most eSIM apps let you add data mid-trip if you run low, so start smaller.
- **Use hotel and café Wi-Fi** for big downloads, video calls and photo backups.
