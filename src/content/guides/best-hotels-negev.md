**Mitzpe Ramon** is built on the rim of the Makhtesh Ramon — Israel's largest erosion crater and one of the country's most dramatic natural landscapes. The town of approximately 9,000 residents sits at 900 metres elevation in the central Negev, with the crater floor 300 metres below. Accommodation here ranges from one of Israel's most iconic luxury hotels to eco-glamping structures with no electricity, a mid-range town-centre chain hotel, a reliable HI hostel, and a growing number of local zimmer guesthouses.

Most visitors to Mitzpe Ramon need a car. The town has bus connections to Beer Sheva, but exploring the crater, driving Route 40, and reaching the Alpaca Farm and remote viewpoints requires independent transport. See the [car rental Israel guide](/car-rental-israel) and the [Mitzpe Ramon visitor guide](/mitzpe-ramon-guide) for the full picture of what to see and do.

## Beresheet Hotel — crater-rim landmark

The **Beresheet Hotel** (Hebrew for "In the Beginning") is the property most associated with Mitzpe Ramon in the travel imagination. Built by Isrotel and positioned at the northern edge of the crater rim, it offers unobstructed views across the Makhtesh Ramon from guest rooms, terraces, restaurants, and its two outdoor pools.

The main pool area sits close to the crater lip, creating the visual effect — heavily photographed — of water appearing to spill over the edge into the crater below. The hotel has a full-service spa, several dining venues including a fine-dining restaurant with panoramic views, and walking trails leading from the property directly to crater-rim viewpoints. Room grades range from standard rooms with partial crater view to suites with private plunge pools on the terrace.

**Who it suits:** Couples on a special-occasion stay, honeymoons, families celebrating b'nai mitzvah trips, and any visitor for whom the desert setting is a primary motivation. The Beresheet is the most requested "splurge" hotel among Israeli domestic travellers and increasingly among international visitors who have read about it in publications like Condé Nast Traveler and National Geographic.

**Booking:** The Beresheet fills far in advance for spring weekends (March–May), Passover, Sukkot, and Hanukkah. For any of these periods, 3–6 months ahead is not excessive. Summer weekday availability is generally better; winter (December–February) has the most room and the best stargazing conditions.

**Rates:** Indicative range only — ₪1,800–₪4,500+/night for standard rooms depending on season and room category, rising sharply at major holidays. All-inclusive breakfast is typically offered as an add-on. Check live rates via Booking.com.

## Selina Ramon — design-forward social hotel

**Selina Ramon** is the Mitzpe Ramon property of the global Selina brand, which targets younger travellers, solo visitors, and digital nomads with a mix of private rooms, co-working facilities, and social programming. It typically sits at a lower price point than the Beresheet while still offering access to crater views, a pool or rooftop area in season, and a more social atmosphere than a standard guesthouse.

Selina properties have a distinctive design aesthetic — salvaged wood, local art, murals — and tend to attract a mix of backpackers upgrading from dorm-only hostels and independent travellers who want design without the full luxury-hotel price. Room categories typically include dorms, twin rooms, and private doubles.

**Who it suits:** Solo travellers, younger couples, digital nomads wanting crater-town Wi-Fi, and anyone who wants a social environment with crater access at a lower price than the Beresheet. The Selina ecosystem also suits travellers on the HI Hostel Pass who want an occasional design-property upgrade.

**Note:** Selina's specific amenities and operational status at this property can evolve seasonally. Check the current room categories and open status via their official site or Booking.com before booking.

## Ramon Inn — mid-range town-centre reliability

The **Ramon Inn** (also an Isrotel property) is Mitzpe Ramon's established mid-range hotel and a solid choice for families, older travellers, and anyone who wants a comfortable room in town without the Beresheet's price point.

Located in the centre of the town rather than on the crater rim itself, the Ramon Inn has a restaurant, pool, and direct access to the town's shops and restaurants. The main crater viewpoint and the visitor centre are a short walk. The hotel was significantly renovated and is consistently rated positively for cleanliness and service.

**Who it suits:** Families with children; travellers who want reliable mid-range infrastructure; visitors arriving late or leaving early who don't need a crater-view room; anyone combining Mitzpe Ramon with Beer Sheva or Eilat who is primarily using the town as a logistical base.

**Rates:** Indicative range approximately ₪600–₪1,100/night depending on season. Spring weekends and holidays still require advance booking — Mitzpe Ramon as a whole fills, not just the Beresheet. Live rates via Booking.com.

## Succah in the Desert — off-grid eco-glamping

**Succah in the Desert** (Succah BaMidbar) is one of Israel's most distinctive accommodation concepts: handcrafted bamboo and recycled-material structures in a desert setting near Mitzpe Ramon, designed for minimal footprint. The original structures have no electricity or running water — lanterns, outdoor composting facilities, and silence. A later "glamping" version with more amenities has also been developed.

The experience is deliberately different from a hotel stay: the appeal is the desert sky overhead, the silence of the Negev plateau, and waking to a sunrise in a landscape that looks genuinely prehistoric. This suits a specific type of traveller who is seeking disconnection and a very different kind of desert immersion.

**Who it suits:** Couples seeking a genuinely unusual night; eco-conscious travellers; photographers chasing Milky Way skies; travellers who have already done Beresheet-tier luxury on a previous visit. **Not suitable** for travellers with mobility challenges, families with very young children, or anyone requiring standard hotel infrastructure.

**Advance booking:** Succah in the Desert has limited capacity and books up quickly for spring and autumn weekends. Book directly via their website.

## HI Beit Noam hostel — budget base

The **HI Beit Noam** (affiliated with Hostelling International Israel) is the main budget option in Mitzpe Ramon and a reliable one. Central in town, it has dorm beds, private rooms, and family rooms, with shared kitchen facilities and typically a basic breakfast option.

The hostel is popular with hikers doing multi-day routes through the crater and the Negev highland trails. It is a straightforward, clean, no-frills base — the draw is the location and price rather than design or facilities.

**Rates:** Dorm beds from approximately ₪120–180/night; private rooms from approximately ₪400–650/night. Israeli school holidays require advance booking even at the hostel level.

## Zimmer and private guesthouses

Beyond the named properties, Mitzpe Ramon has a growing inventory of private zimmer — Israeli-style B&B rooms, often with a private entrance and terrace — operated by local families and small operators. These typically run ₪350–₪700 per room/night, offer a more personal experience than a chain hotel, and are often very well positioned relative to the crater viewpoints.

Search on Booking.com or Airbnb filtering to Mitzpe Ramon specifically; many local operators list on both platforms. Quality varies — read reviews, confirm inclusions (breakfast, towels, air conditioning for summer), and confirm the property's exact distance from the crater rim.

## Sde Boker area — alternative Negev base

If your Negev itinerary prioritises the **Sde Boker / Ein Avdat** area (Ben-Gurion's desert home, the dramatic white-cliff canyon of Nahal Zin, and the Avdat Nabataean ruins on Route 40), the **Kibbutz Sde Boker guesthouse** and the **Midreshet Ben-Gurion campus** accommodation are alternatives to basing in Mitzpe Ramon itself. These are approximately 30 kilometres north of Mitzpe Ramon, and offer a quieter, more academic-campus environment surrounded by Negev highland scenery.

Sde Boker does not have the crater-view draw or the concentration of restaurants and services of Mitzpe Ramon, but for visitors whose primary interest is the Ben-Gurion heritage sites and the Ein Avdat canyon hike, it removes the 30km morning drive.

For the full guide to what to see and do in this area, see the [Sde Boker and Ein Avdat guide](/sde-boker-ein-avdat-guide).

## Seasonal notes and booking timing

**Spring (March–May):** Peak season for the Negev. Temperatures are ideal for hiking (15–25°C days), the Darom Adom anemone bloom may extend into early March further north, wildflowers carpet the Negev hills, and the crater is at its most comfortable. Israeli families flood the Mitzpe Ramon hotels at every Passover and Shabbat weekend. Book Beresheet 4–6 months ahead; Ramon Inn 2–3 months; zimmer and hostel 1–2 months.

**Autumn (September–October):** Second peak season, with excellent temperatures and Sukkot holiday demand. The Beresheet fills completely at Sukkot. Stargazing conditions are excellent in October.

**Summer (July–August):** Crater midday temperatures reach 35–42°C and exposed hiking is not recommended between 10am and 4pm. The Beresheet's pools and spa are a major draw. Early-morning crater drives and sunset hikes are still excellent. Accommodation availability is better than spring; accommodation prices rise at school holiday weeks.

**Winter (December–February):** The desert has cold nights (sometimes below 5°C or lower) and occasional frost on the crater rim. Daytime hiking is very comfortable. The Beresheet is at its most affordable and the best months for the IDA dark-sky experience. The hostel and zimmer fill at Hanukkah; otherwise winter is the lowest-demand period.

All prices quoted are indicative ranges only — live rates change daily. Check Booking.com for current availability and pricing at your specific dates.
