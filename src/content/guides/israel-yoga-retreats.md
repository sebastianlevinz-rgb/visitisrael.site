Israel is an unlikely candidate for the title of retreat destination — yet its landscape and spiritual depth make it a natural one. The Arava desert, one of the world's most geologically ancient places, hosts luxury eco-retreats and off-grid meditation communities within a few kilometres of each other. The Galilee hillsides, where silence settles quickly in the evening, draw teachers of both Jewish contemplative tradition and contemporary mindfulness practice. And the country's sheer density of sacred landscapes — contested, layered, historically saturated — lends even an ordinary walk a quality that deliberate retreat centres elsewhere work hard to manufacture.

This guide covers the main retreat options honestly: what each centre actually is, what style of practice it offers, and how to reach and book it. All prices are given as ranges only — rates vary seasonally and change annually; always verify directly with the centre.

---

## Arava & southern Negev retreat centres

The Arava valley and the southern Negev form the heartland of Israel's retreat scene. The climate is arid and extreme, the stars exceptional, and the isolation genuine.

### Six Senses Shaharut

The flagship luxury property in Israel's retreat landscape occupies a dramatic plateau above the Arava valley near Eilat, at an elevation that softens the desert heat. Six Senses Shaharut runs a full spa and Ayurveda programme alongside yoga sessions, guided desert hikes, and stargazing at altitude. The design integrates local basalt and sandstone — the property has a strong sense of place rather than the generic luxury hotel feel. Rates are at the upper end of the Israeli market; verify current prices and packages directly with Six Senses.

Best for: special-occasion or honeymoon wellness stays; Ayurveda or detox programmes; luxury travellers who want genuine desert isolation.

### Desert Ashram

Located in the Negev, the Desert Ashram runs workshops and programmes in Osho-lineage meditation practices — dynamic meditation, movement-based group processes, and silence retreats. It also hosts an annual desert festival that draws practitioners from across Israel and internationally. The setting is raw and intentional; the programme style is expressive and body-centred rather than silent or devotional. Read about the Osho tradition before booking if it is unfamiliar — the approach is distinctive.

Best for: those already familiar with Osho or active-meditation traditions; festival-style gatherings; practitioners who want intensity and community over luxury.

### Moa Oasis

Moa Oasis is a small eco-retreat on the historic Nabataean incense route in the central Negev highlands. The Nabataean civilisation built a network of caravanserai along these ancient trade routes, and Moa sits within this landscape context. It runs yoga and workshop programmes alongside accommodation in simple eco-structures. The combination of archaeological atmosphere, desert quiet, and contemplative programming makes it unusual. Book via the Moa website directly; advance notice is required.

### Ne'ot Semadar

Ne'ot Semadar is a community and eco-oasis in the remote southern Arava, not far from the Jordanian border. It operates as an intentional living community and produces wines, olive oil, and organic goods. Yoga and movement workshops are offered to guests, and the community ethos — sustainable agriculture, handcraft, deliberate pace — creates an environment that acts as a retreat in itself even without a formal programme. Stays and workshops bookable via the Ne'ot Semadar site.

### Lev Hamidbar

Lev Hamidbar ('Heart of the Desert') is a smaller retreat and workshop centre in the Arava that runs weekend and week-long programmes in yoga, somatic practices, and nature-based contemplation. Its proximity to both Eilat and the Timna geological park makes it a natural pairing with a Timna or Red Sea visit.

## Galilee retreat centres

The lower Galilee, overlooking the Sea of Galilee, provides a softer and more accessible landscape for retreat programmes — greener, more temperate, and within reach of Tiberias and its transport connections.

### Mitzpe Alummot

Mitzpe Alummot is a retreat and raw-food centre in the lower Galilee with panoramic views over the Sea of Galilee. It combines a plant-based and raw-food approach with yoga and body-awareness practices. The setting — elevated hillside, sea below, orchards around — makes it a distinctive destination for practitioners who combine physical and dietary practice. It caters to both residential retreats and shorter day or weekend visits.

### Or HaLev

Or HaLev ('Light of the Heart') focuses on Jewish-rooted contemplative practice: Shabbat retreat weekends, silent retreats informed by Hasidic and Mussar tradition, and programmes combining study, prayer and mindfulness. It welcomes all levels of Jewish background and familiarity — the emphasis is on interior practice, not halachic observance. A two- or three-day Shabbat retreat at Or HaLev is one of the more unusual and culturally specific experiences available in Israel.

## How to choose and book

**By landscape preference:** Negev/Arava if you want raw desert, silence and geological extremity; Galilee if you prefer green hills, cooler temperatures, and proximity to historical sites.

**By style:** luxury eco-resort (Six Senses Shaharut); community/ashram (Desert Ashram, Ne'ot Semadar); Jewish-spiritual (Or HaLev); raw-food holistic (Mitzpe Alummot); eco-archaeology (Moa Oasis).

**By season:** spring and autumn for all Negev/Arava centres; Galilee is comfortable year-round except midsummer; winter in the Negev is cool and spectacular but verify programme availability.

**Booking platforms:** Many Israel retreat centres list on bookretreats.com and bookyogaretreats.com. Six Senses Shaharut is bookable direct. Others (Desert Ashram, Ne'ot Semadar, Moa Oasis, Or HaLev) are best booked through their own sites. Always confirm current programme dates and availability by email or phone before committing.

**Transport:** a rental car is essential for all Arava/Negev centres. [Compare car hire from Tel Aviv and Ben Gurion Airport](/car-rental-israel) — most bookings include free cancellation.

## Practical notes

**Prices:** Israel retreat prices span an enormous range — from a few hundred shekels for a weekend workshop at a community centre to several thousand US dollars per night for a luxury spa programme. This guide gives no specific prices in the body copy because they change seasonally and with exchange rates. Treat any figure as approximate and verify directly.

**Diet:** most retreat centres default to vegetarian or vegan meals. Six Senses Shaharut can accommodate most dietary requirements. Confirm your needs when booking.

**Language:** most programmes at the centres above offer at least some English-language content. Or HaLev programmes are sometimes Hebrew-only — confirm before booking if English access is important.

**Regional security context:** the Arava and southern Negev are far from Israel's northern and Gaza borders and experience no direct conflict impact in normal times. Always check your home government's current travel advisory before planning any Israel travel: [US State Dept](https://travel.state.gov), [UK FCDO](https://www.gov.uk/foreign-travel-advice/israel), [Australian Smartraveller](https://www.smartraveller.gov.au/destinations/middle-east/israel-and-occupied-palestinian-territories).
