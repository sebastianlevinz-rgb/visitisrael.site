Petra and Wadi Rum are the two most visited sites in Jordan — and from [Eilat](/eilat), both are within reach on a single overnight trip. A 2-day itinerary gets you a sunset jeep safari in Wadi Rum and a full day at Petra. Three days adds breathing room: a slower Wadi Rum morning, time for the Monastery hike, or a night in [Aqaba](/aqaba-from-eilat) on the Red Sea.

This guide covers both formats, the costs, the Jordan Pass question, and the desert logistics that most tour pages skip.

---

## Why go from Eilat?

The [Yitzhak Rabin / Wadi Araba border crossing](/border-crossings) sits just 5 km north of central Eilat — a 10-minute taxi ride. Once across, Wadi Rum is about 3.5 hours north and Petra is 3.5 hours northeast. The border is open daily (typically 06:30–20:00; verify current hours before travel) and the crossing takes 45–90 minutes each way on a normal day.

Eilat is the logical launch point if your trip is centred on Israel. If you're touring Jordan on its own or flying into Amman, Petra and Wadi Rum are better reached via the King's Highway or Desert Highway from the north — the [Petra from Eilat vs Amman comparison](/petra-from-eilat-vs-amman) covers that decision in detail.

## Jordan Pass: buy before you cross

The [Jordan Pass](/jordan-pass-guide) is the first thing to sort before your trip. Purchased at jordanpass.jo, it bundles:

- **Jordanian visa fee** (JD 40 / ~$55) — waived for visitors who stay at least one night
- **Petra entry** (1, 2, or 3 days: JD 50 / 55 / 60 separately)
- **Wadi Rum Visitor Centre** (~JD 5)
- 40+ additional Jordan sites

For a 2-day trip with one Petra day, the pass saves roughly JD 25–35 versus paying separately. Buy it online before leaving Israel — it cannot be purchased at the border crossing.

**Day-tripper note:** The visa waiver only applies if you stay at least one night in Jordan. Day-trippers from Eilat who return the same day pay the JD 40 visa fee separately on top of the pass, which reduces the saving. An overnight Bedouin camp (included in most tour packages) makes the pass unambiguously the better deal.

## 2-day itinerary: Wadi Rum overnight → Petra

The classic format. Tight but very doable.

### Day 1 — Wadi Rum

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 07:30 | Cross Wadi Araba border into Aqaba |
| 09:30 | Border cleared; transfer begins (~3.5 hrs to Wadi Rum) |
| 13:00 | Arrive Wadi Rum village; lunch |
| 14:00–18:00 | 4-hour jeep safari: Lawrence's Spring, Khazali Canyon, Um Sabatah dune |
| 18:30 | Sunset at the dunes — the single best moment of the trip |
| 19:30 | Dinner at camp (included in most packages) |
| Night | Overnight Bedouin camp |

Camps range from basic tents (hot shower, shared bathrooms, ~JD 40/night per person) to bubble pods or "Martian Domes" with panoramic views (~JD 100–160/night). Most mid-range organised tours include a comfortable camp with private bathrooms and electricity.

### Day 2 — Petra

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 07:00 | Early start from camp (1.5 hrs to Petra) |
| 08:30 | Arrive Petra Visitor Centre; collect map |
| 09:00–15:00 | Full Petra day: Siq → Treasury (Al-Khazneh) → Street of Facades → Theatre → Royal Tombs |
| Optional | If legs hold: add the Monastery (Al-Deir) — 800-step climb, the site's most dramatic monument after the Treasury; allow 90 minutes extra |
| 15:30 | Depart Petra for the border (~2 hrs) |
| 18:30 | Re-enter Israel at Wadi Araba |
| 19:00 | Back in Eilat |

**Honest Petra expectation:** The walk from the Visitor Centre to the Treasury through the Siq takes 45 minutes each way and covers about 4 km. The full Petra site is much larger — the Monastery and the High Place of Sacrifice add several kilometres and significant elevation. Comfortable walking shoes are non-negotiable.

## 3-day itinerary: the unhurried version

An extra day removes the pressure and opens up two options.

### Option A — Aqaba overnight (add Day 0 or Day 3)

[Aqaba](/aqaba-from-eilat) is Jordan's Red Sea resort city, 15 minutes from the border. Adding a night here lets you:
- Snorkel the South Beach coral gardens (equipment hire on the beach)
- Visit Al-Aqabah Mamluk Castle (free with Jordan Pass)
- Eat at the waterfront fish restaurants without watching the clock

Combine as: **Day 1** Eilat → Aqaba (overnight) / **Day 2** Aqaba → Wadi Rum (overnight camp) / **Day 3** Wadi Rum → Petra → Eilat.

### Option B — Extra Petra day

Stay two nights on the Jordan side (Day 1 Wadi Rum, Day 2 in Wadi Musa / Petra gateway). A second Petra morning covers what Day 1 couldn't:

- The **Monastery (Al-Deir)** — 800 rock-carved steps, a wider facade than the Treasury, and far fewer crowds in the early morning
- The **High Place of Sacrifice** — hilltop altar with panoramic views over the Siq
- The **Colonnaded Street** and Byzantine church mosaics

A Jordan Pass with a 2-day Petra ticket (JD 55) pays for itself easily.

## Package vs DIY: what it costs

| | 2-Day Package (per person) | 2-Day DIY |
|---|---|---|
| **Border transfer** | Included | Taxi to border ~₪50; Jordanian taxi onward ~JD 60–80 |
| **Wadi Rum jeep safari** | Included | ~JD 40–70 per person (book direct with Rum Stars, Beduin Roads, etc.) |
| **Camp (B&B)** | Included | ~JD 40–70 per person |
| **Transfer to Petra** | Included | Jordanian taxi ~JD 60 |
| **Jordan Pass** | Usually not included — buy separately | ~JD 75 (1-day Petra) |
| **Guide at Petra** | Usually optional add-on | Optional, ~JD 30–60 |
| **Total estimate** | **$519–$595/person** (GYG/Viator benchmark) | **~$300–420/person** plus your own coordination |

**When packages win:** Timing is tight on a 2-day trip. A missed transfer or a long border queue can cascade. Packages absorb that risk — the operator handles the scheduling, the camp booking, and the border wait. For most travellers on a first visit, the ~$100–150 premium is worth it.

**When DIY works:** If you're a confident independent traveller, have visited Jordan before, or are joining a multi-day Jordan trip (where the costs spread further), DIY is perfectly feasible.

## What to pack for the desert

- **Warm layer.** Wadi Rum nights are cold year-round; in winter they can reach 5°C. A fleece or down jacket is not optional.
- **Comfortable walking shoes.** Petra covers several kilometres on uneven stone paths; open sandals are a bad idea.
- **Sun protection.** Both sites are fully exposed. SPF 50+, a wide-brim hat, and sunglasses for the desert light.
- **Water.** Petra and Wadi Rum have vendors, but carry at least 1.5 litres from the start of each day.
- **Cash in Jordanian dinars.** Take JD from the Aqaba ATMs or pre-exchange at Eilat — border kiosks offer poor rates.
- **Comfortable camp clothes.** Wadi Rum camps are casual but evenings are social; leave the hiking boots on but bring a warmer top.

## Practicalities

**Border hours.** Wadi Araba / Yitzhak Rabin crossing is open daily (06:30–20:00 approximately — verify with your tour operator or Israeli MFA before travel). This is the crossing to use; do not confuse with the Allenby / King Hussein Bridge crossing further north.

**ETA-IL.** You need a valid Israeli ETA-IL (or visa) to re-enter Israel after Jordan. If your nationality requires ETA-IL, ensure it is active — the ETA-IL obtained when you originally entered Israel remains valid for its duration; you do not need to reapply just for the Jordan border run, but check your entry document is still valid.

**Passport stamps.** Israel no longer stamps passports at the Wadi Araba crossing (it uses a removable border slip). Jordan stamps your passport. If you have concerns about dual-stamp restrictions onward to certain countries, consult the relevant embassy before travel.

**Phone coverage.** Israeli SIM data does not work in Jordan. A cheap Zain or YCOM Jordanian SIM is available in Aqaba (~JD 5) or you can rent a pocket Wi-Fi from some tour operators.
