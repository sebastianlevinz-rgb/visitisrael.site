Israel's craft spirits story begins, like so many good stories, with an unlikely premise. Until 2012, there was no whisky distillery in the country. The founder of M&H (Milk & Honey) Distillery, an Israeli entrepreneur with a background in olive oil and an obsession with Scotch, looked at Israel's abundant sunshine, its coastal sea air, and its access to premium barley, and made a bet that the Mediterranean could produce something new in single malt. A decade later, his distillery had won the World's Best Single Malt at the World Whiskies Awards. The Israeli craft spirits industry was no longer a curiosity — it was a destination.

This guide covers the full picture: Israeli whisky, craft gin, artisan arak, and where to taste all of it.

## M&H Distillery — Israel's flagship spirits producer

**M&H (Milk & Honey) Distillery** is Tel Aviv's most internationally significant spirits producer and the starting point for any serious Israeli spirits itinerary. Founded in 2012 and releasing its first official whisky in 2017, M&H has compressed a century of whisky-country brand-building into less than a decade — driven primarily by the quality of what's in the bottle.

The distillery is located in Tel Aviv's northern industrial district. Visits are by appointment only (Monday to Friday; book via milkandhoneydistillery.com), and include:

- **Production floor tour** — copper pot stills, the grain room, the process explained in English by knowledgeable guides
- **Barrel warehouse** — rows of maturing casks, including STR (shaved, toasted, re-charred) wine casks, ex-bourbon barrels and ex-sherry casks; the heat of the warehouse is immediately striking — this is not a cool Scottish dunnage
- **Guided tasting** — approximately 90 minutes total; includes core range expressions plus, when available, limited Apex or Elements series releases

**What to taste:**

| Expression | Character |
|---|---|
| M&H Classic | Entry point; approachable, lightly peated, tropical fruit notes |
| M&H Elements Sherry Cask | Rich, dried fruit, chocolate; signature non-peated expression |
| M&H Elements Peated | Smokier; balances peat with Mediterranean warmth |
| M&H Apex Series | Limited cask-strength releases; the most collector-sought expressions |

> **Honesty note:** The Apex series sells out quickly — some expressions are only available at the distillery or via the website. Retail prices given in this guide are indicative; they change with production volumes and exchange rates. The distillery is not set up for walk-in visitors — advance booking is essential.

**Getting there:** The distillery is in the Har Hotzvim area of northern Tel Aviv. Uber/Gett from central Tel Aviv runs approximately ₪35–45; the Tel Aviv Light Rail connects to nearby stops. A taxi back via Jaffa Old Town makes a natural evening continuation.

## Israeli craft gin

The craft gin category arrived in Israel in earnest around 2015–2018, following the global gin renaissance. Several small-batch Israeli gins are now worth seeking out:

**Golani Gin** — produced in the north and using botanicals from the Golan Heights region, including sage, wild herbs and regional citrus. Available at specialist spirits shops in Tel Aviv and Jerusalem; found occasionally at upscale hotel bars.

**Levant Gin** — a Mediterranean botanical profile: carob pods, pomelo peel, sage and local herbs alongside standard juniper-led gin botanicals. Small-batch production means availability is inconsistent; specialist bottle shops in Tel Aviv's Florentin and central Jerusalem are the most reliable sources.

**Dead Sea Gin** — an unusual concept: a gin positioned around the unique mineral character of its water source. More of a conversation piece than the most refined expression, but worth trying for the concept alone.

The best single place to explore Israeli craft gin is at a specialist spirits bar in Tel Aviv — the staff at Dr. Whisky Bar can steer you to current availability. Beer Bazaar locations also carry a selection of Israeli craft spirits alongside their extensive craft beer range.

## Israeli arak — the traditional spirit

Before whisky, before gin, there was **arak**. Israel's traditional anise spirit is part of the wider Levantine arak family — produced from grapes and flavoured with anise seeds, it turns milky white when diluted with water and ice, and is the canonical accompaniment to mezze.

Israeli arak is closely related to Lebanese arak; subtle differences exist in grape varieties and local production traditions, but both share the same cultural DNA. The most widely available Israeli arak brands are Aluf HaArak and the less commercially distributed boutique versions from small producers in Jaffa and Jerusalem.

**How to drink arak:** dilute roughly 1:2 with cold water, add ice, drink alongside hummus, baba ghanoush, and grilled meats. Never add ice before water — the arak goes cloudy (a process called louching) more gracefully when diluted first. Order it at any traditional mezze restaurant, particularly in East Jerusalem, Akko, Jaffa or Haifa's Arab neighborhoods.

## Where to taste Israeli craft spirits in Tel Aviv

**Dr. Whisky Bar** (Ibn Gavirol Street, Tel Aviv) is the essential address for anyone who wants to explore Israeli whisky alongside international comparisons. The bar carries the full M&H core range and rotating Apex expressions alongside an impressive international selection — the staff can guide you through Israel vs. Scotland vs. Japan tasting comparisons that contextualize how the Mediterranean climate shapes the Israeli style. Open evenings; closed Friday night through Saturday (Shabbat).

**Hacarmel 10** (near Carmel Market) — a wine bar that takes its spirits selection seriously; stocks Israeli craft spirits alongside natural wines and has a kitchen for food pairing.

**Beer Bazaar** — all locations carry a selection of Israeli craft spirits alongside their craft beer programme. The Carmel Market flagship has the most comprehensive spirits shelf. Worth noting for visitors who want to try Israeli craft spirits without making a separate bar visit.

**Hotel bars:** The Norman, The Setai Jaffa and several boutique hotels in the Rothschild corridor now carry Israeli craft spirits on their cocktail menus — useful if you're staying at one of these properties.

## Where to taste beyond Tel Aviv

**Jerusalem:** Select hotel bars in the luxury tier carry M&H whisky. The Machane Yehuda market area has a small bar scene that stocks Israeli craft spirits; the Machneyuda restaurant has been known to use Israeli arak in cocktails. The city closes earlier than Tel Aviv and Shabbat observance is more widespread — plan accordingly.

**Haifa:** A growing cocktail and spirits bar scene centred on Masada Street in the lower city. Not as extensive as Tel Aviv but worth checking for visiting the Golan Heights whisky and spirits producers in the north (Golan Heights Winery produces brandy expressions; smaller Golan distillers may offer tastings — verify availability in advance).

## Buying Israeli spirits to take home

**Ben Gurion Airport Terminal 3 duty-free** is the most reliable single location for the widest M&H range — including some Israel-exclusive expressions that are not available for export. Allow time before your flight. The duty-free whisky section has expanded significantly since 2022 as Israeli whisky has gained international recognition.

**Specialist bottle shops** in Tel Aviv (Florentin, Dizengoff area) and Jerusalem (near Mahane Yehuda) carry the M&H core range plus occasional craft gin labels. Staff at specialist shops tend to be knowledgeable — ask what's new and what they'd recommend for your palate.

**Customs allowances:** Most Western countries allow 1 litre of spirits duty-free per person. If you want to bring home more than one bottle, check your home country's customs rules before buying; enforcement varies but the official limit is typically 1L.

## Practical notes

**Kosher:** M&H Distillery is kosher-certified. Some craft gin and arak producers are not — verify if this matters for your situation.

**Shabbat closures:** Most specialist spirits bars in Tel Aviv close Friday afternoon (roughly 3–4 pm) and reopen Saturday night after Shabbat ends. Plan your tasting route for Sunday through Thursday for maximum availability.

**Prices:** M&H core range expressions retail at approximately ₪180–400 in Israel. Apex and limited-edition releases command higher prices and sell out. Tasting tours at the distillery are priced on the distillery website; do not depend on prices in this guide — verify current pricing directly.

**Language:** English is universally spoken at M&H Distillery and at specialist spirits bars in Tel Aviv. Guided tours at the distillery are available in English.
