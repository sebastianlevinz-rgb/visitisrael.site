Israel is compact and densely packed with experiences — but the combination of climates, religious sites, Dead Sea chemistry and Ben Gurion Airport security creates a distinctive packing challenge. This guide covers what actually matters, by season and trip type.

## Luggage strategy

**One carry-on is almost always the right answer.** Ben Gurion Airport security is among the most thorough in the world — checked bags add time on both arrival and departure. A 40-litre carry-on and a 20-litre day pack handles two weeks comfortably for most travellers.

Exception: if you are hiking the [Israel National Trail](/hiking-in-israel) or multi-day Galilee routes, a 65-litre pack with a frame is worth the checked-bag overhead. For a city-and-beaches trip, it is not.

**Laundry is easy.** Jerusalem, Tel Aviv, Haifa and Eilat all have laundromats or self-service machines. Most guesthouses and hostels offer laundry. Pack for 5–7 days and plan one wash cycle on a 10–14 day trip.

## Documents and essentials

- **Passport** valid for at least six months beyond your planned departure date.
- **ETA-IL** (if applicable) — since January 2025, most visa-exempt travellers must obtain an Electronic Travel Authorisation before boarding. Apply online via the Israeli PIBA portal at least 72 hours ahead. See our [visa and ETA-IL guide](/visa-information) for step-by-step instructions.
- **Travel insurance documents** — Israel's healthcare is excellent and expensive. See our [Israel travel insurance guide](/israel-travel-insurance) for what a good policy covers.
- **Type H plug adaptor** — three flat prongs in a Y shape, 230V. Non-negotiable unless you only bring USB-C devices with their own bricks.
- **Copies of key documents** — photograph your passport, insurance policy and ETA-IL and email them to yourself so you can access them if originals are lost.

## Clothing by season

### Summer (June–September)

Israel in summer is hot across the country, intense at the Dead Sea and in Eilat (regularly above 40°C). The Mediterranean coast (Tel Aviv, Haifa) stays in the low-to-mid 30s with a sea breeze — bearable, but pack accordingly.

- **Linen or moisture-wicking fabrics** — cotton is fine for casual days; linen breathes better in extreme heat.
- **Sun hat with a brim** — essential, not optional. UV intensity in Israel is significant year-round and peaks in summer.
- **Sunglasses with UV400 protection.**
- **Light cardigan or long-sleeve layer** — air conditioning in Israeli restaurants and malls is aggressive. Bring one layer you can add indoors.
- **Scarf or shawl** for religious sites — see [holy site dress code guide](/holy-sites-dress-code-etiquette) for site-specific rules.
- **Swimsuit × 2** — one for the Mediterranean or Red Sea; one dedicated old swimsuit for the Dead Sea (salt bleaches and destroys elastic).
- **Old sandals for the Dead Sea** — Dead Sea salt crystals are sharp on bare feet; brine destroys regular footwear quickly. Wear something you can discard.

### Spring (March–May) and Autumn (September–November)

The optimal travel seasons. Temperatures are comfortable across the country — typically 18–26°C — with occasional rain in spring.

- **Light layers** — mornings can be cool, afternoons warm. A packable down jacket handles evenings in Jerusalem (800m elevation).
- **Light rain jacket** — April especially can bring brief showers in the north (Galilee, Golan) and Jerusalem.
- **Walking shoes** — spring wildflowers make hiking trails particularly rewarding; sturdy shoes worth the weight.

### Winter (November–March)

Eilat and the Dead Sea region stay warm (20–25°C daytime). Jerusalem can drop below freezing and occasionally sees snow — rare but real. Galilee and Golan can be cold and wet.

- **Layering system** — a base layer, mid-layer fleece and a waterproof shell covers the range.
- **Rain jacket** — essential for Jerusalem and the north.
- **Warm hat and gloves** — if visiting Jerusalem December–February.
- Eilat winter packing is summer packing with a light jacket for evenings.

## Footwear

Three pairs covers most trips:

1. **Comfortable walking shoes** — well-worn, not new. Jerusalem's Old City cobblestones will blister untested feet fast.
2. **Hiking boots or trail shoes** — for [Masada](/masada-visitor-guide), [hiking in Israel](/hiking-in-israel) and nature trails. Lightweight trail runners work for most marked trails; boots for Wadi Qelt or the Golan rim.
3. **Old sandals or cheap flip-flops** — dedicated Dead Sea footwear. Leave them at the beach when you're done.

Slip-on shoes are useful for days that include mosque visits, where footwear is removed at the entrance.

## Health and hygiene

- **Sunscreen SPF 50+** — Israeli pharmacies stock it, but it is expensive by international standards. Bring a full supply.
- **After-sun or moisturiser** — Dead Sea air is hyper-dry; the mineral content can dehydrate skin. A barrier moisturiser after a float session is not a luxury.
- **Insect repellent** — less critical in cities; useful in the Galilee, Golan and near the Jordan River.
- **Prescription medications in original labelled packaging** — Israeli customs allows a reasonable traveller's supply. Carry a copy of the prescription for anything controlled.
- **Basic first-aid kit** — blister plasters especially. Israeli pharmacies (בֵּית מִרְקַחַת) are widespread and well-stocked if you need anything more.
- **EHIC and EU health cards** — not recognised in Israel. Comprehensive travel insurance is the practical substitute. See [Israel travel insurance](/israel-travel-insurance).

## Electronics

- **Type H adaptor** — see Documents section above.
- **Portable power bank** — must travel in carry-on, not checked luggage. Ben Gurion security checks power bank watt-hour ratings; standard 20,000 mAh banks (72 Wh) pass without issue.
- **eSIM or SIM plan** — see our [Israel eSIM guide](/israel-esim) before departure. Israeli data is cheap; roaming is expensive.
- **Camera** — Israel is extraordinarily photogenic. For guidance on where to shoot, see [best photography spots in Israel](/israel-photography-guide).
- **Offline maps downloaded** — Google Maps and Maps.me offline map packages cover Israel and work well even in areas with patchy signal (parts of the Negev and Golan).

## What NOT to bring

- **Military or tactical gear** — camouflage patterns, surplus vests and gear resembling security equipment attract extended scrutiny at Ben Gurion. Dress as a civilian.
- **Loose spare lithium batteries in checked luggage** — most airlines prohibit spare lithium batteries in hold baggage. Carry them in your personal item.
- **Recreational cannabis** — illegal in Israel. Airport security at Ben Gurion is thorough and experienced.
- **New shoes** — break them in at home first. Old-city cobblestones are unforgiving on day one.
- **Expensive jewellery you would miss** — Jerusalem's Old City markets and crowded attractions are the rare spots where opportunistic theft occurs. Leave irreplaceable items at home or in the hotel safe.

## Packing by trip type

**City-and-culture trip (Tel Aviv + Jerusalem):** carry-on only; walking shoes; one smart-casual outfit for better restaurants; scarf for religious sites; swimsuit for Tel Aviv beach.

**Dead Sea + Masada day trip:** old sandals; old swimsuit; 2L water minimum; sunscreen; light layers for the Masada pre-dawn hike.

**Galilee hiking route:** 30L day pack; hiking boots; trekking poles (optional but useful on descents); iodine tablets or filter (springs not guaranteed safe without treatment); hydration bladder.

**Eilat Red Sea diving:** standard dive kit (mask/fins/wetsuit if you have them) or rent locally; BC and regulator rental available at all major dive centres. Coral Beach Nature Reserve entry is separate from dive centres — bring the [Israel National Parks Pass](/israel-national-parks-pass) if you are visiting multiple reserves.
