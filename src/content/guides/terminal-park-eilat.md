**Terminal Park Eilat** is the new entertainment complex built on the site of the city's decommissioned Aqaba Airport — a flat, formerly disused expanse of southern Eilat that has been transformed into a leisure destination anchored by a large Ferris wheel and family attractions. The park adds a distinct evening entertainment option to a city that is already well served by beaches, snorkeling, and desert excursions — it is not a replacement for any of those but a supplement, particularly useful for families looking for an activity after a long beach or water day.

---

## What to expect at the park

### The Ferris wheel

The centrepiece of Terminal Park is a large Ferris wheel — one of the tallest structures in this part of Eilat — offering aerial views in four directions: the Red Sea and Aqaba waterfront to the south and west, the Eilat Mountains rising immediately to the north, the Jordanian city of Aqaba across the Gulf, and the faint outline of the Saudi Arabian coast to the east on clear days. This is a genuinely distinctive viewpoint that differs from anything achievable from ground level in the city.

The ride runs throughout the park's operating hours. Verify current prices and wait times at the entrance kiosk.

### Rides and family attractions

Beyond the Ferris wheel, the park includes additional amusement rides and children's play areas. The exact lineup of attractions and their specifications changes as the park expands — check eilat.city for the current attraction roster. The development phase includes a street market component, which may still be under construction or in phased opening during your visit.

### Food and drinks

Food vendors are present throughout the park. The evening transformation of the site — with lights, food stalls operating, and the waterfront backdrop — makes this a reasonable dinner-and-entertainment evening option, particularly for families.

## Combining Terminal Park with an Eilat itinerary

Terminal Park works best as an evening component of a day that started with something more physically active. A natural combination for families:

- **Morning**: Coral Beach Nature Reserve for snorkeling on the reef — the best free activity in Eilat for swimmers; brings reef-safe sunscreen, as standard sunscreen is prohibited inside the reserve. See the [Eilat beach guide](/eilat-beach-guide) for a full beach comparison.
- **Afternoon**: Underwater Observatory Marine Park (3 km south on the coastal road) or Dolphin Reef observation session.
- **Evening**: Terminal Park for the Ferris wheel, family rides, and dinner at one of the park food vendors or the nearby port-area restaurants.

For a day combining the Eilat Mountains rather than the sea:

- **Morning–midday**: Red Canyon slot canyon hike (20 km northwest, 1.5–2 hours; free; requires early start in summer) — see the [Red Canyon guide](/red-canyon-eilat).
- **Afternoon**: Return to North Beach for swimming and cooling down.
- **Evening**: Terminal Park.

If you are looking for accommodation close to both the beach and Terminal Park, the North Beach resort strip is the most convenient base. See the [Eilat hotels guide](/eilat-hotels-guide) for hotel options by tier and location.

## Getting to Eilat

Most domestic visitors fly: El Al, Arkia and Israir serve Ramon Airport (ETM), 18 km north of central Eilat, with 50-minute flights from Tel Aviv. International visitors typically connect via Ben Gurion Airport. Driving from Tel Aviv takes 4–5 hours via Route 90 (Dead Sea road) or Route 40 (through the Negev highlands via Mitzpe Ramon). There is no train to Eilat.
