**Ashdod is Israel's primary southern cruise port** — and arguably the more privileged of the two (Haifa is the northern port), because it puts Jerusalem within one hour. That single geographic fact shapes everything about Ashdod shore excursions: the vast majority of cruise passengers head straight for the Old City, and the rest divide between the Dead Sea and Tel Aviv.

This guide covers every practical dimension of planning your Ashdod day — destinations, timing, transport options, and what to prioritise if you have limited hours ashore. ## Jerusalem from Ashdod — the standout excursion

Jerusalem is the first choice for most Ashdod cruise passengers — and for good reason. The Old City is only an hour from the port, holds three of the world's great religious sites side by side, and can deliver a genuinely profound day even in 5–6 hours.

**What to see in a cruise day (4–5 hrs in the city):**

- **Western Wall (Kotel)** — Open to all; men and women in separate prayer sections; no camera restriction outside prayer times; head covering provided at entrance
- **Church of the Holy Sepulchre** — Christian pilgrimage anchor; built over the traditional site of the Crucifixion and Resurrection; free entry; queue moves steadily
- **Four quarters walk** — Jewish, Christian, Muslim and Armenian quarters; the narrow stone lanes of the souk, the Cardo, Muristan and Jaffa Gate area can be covered on foot in 2–3 hours
- **Tower of David Museum** — optional; hilltop citadel with the best panoramic view of the Old City; allow 1.5 hours

**Transport options:**

| Option | Time | Cost (approx) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Private guide + car** | 1 hr each way | ₪800–1,200/car/day | Includes parking; most flexible; guide adds value in the Old City |
| **Shared mini-coach excursion** | 1 hr each way | Ship price or $60–130/person | Ship timing guarantee; English guide; fixed route |
| **Private taxi** | 45–60 min | ~₪250–350 one-way | Pre-arrange return; negotiate fixed price; no meter |
| **Bus (Route 405/436)** | 70–90 min total | ~₪15 | Taxi to Ashdod bus station + 60-min bus; slower, cheap option for confident travellers |

**Key timing rules:**

1. **Allow 2 hours to return to the ship** — Jerusalem traffic, especially Sunday mornings and Friday afternoons, is unpredictable. A 4 pm departure from Jerusalem puts you at port by 5:15 pm with buffer.
2. **Jerusalem is not self-drive friendly** — Parking near the Old City is limited and stressful. A guide with a minibus or taxi with a knowledgeable driver solves this.
3. **Old City security note** — All visitors pass through metal detectors at the gates; keep bags light and avoid bringing penknives.

## Dead Sea & Masada from Ashdod

The Dead Sea sits about 90–105 km from Ashdod — roughly 1.5–2 hours by car. This means a solo Dead Sea + Masada day is a long circuit, but it's one of Israel's most memorable experiences and draws a large number of Ashdod cruise passengers.

**Dead Sea (Ein Bokek beach):**
- Free public beach at Ein Bokek
- The float needs no skill — the salt concentration (34%) makes sinking physically impossible
- 15–20 minutes in the water is enough; rinse thoroughly with fresh water after
- Do not float if you have cuts or shaved recently — salt stings acutely
- Bring an old swimsuit (salt bleaches and damages fabric) and water shoes (salt crystals are sharp underfoot)

**Masada:**
- Ancient Herodian mountain fortress (UNESCO World Heritage Site)
- Take the cable car up (3 minutes, avoids a strenuous 45-minute hike)
- The summit plateau, palace ruins and Roman siege wall are remarkable
- Allow 1.5–2 hours on site

**Combining both in one cruise day:**

A Masada + Dead Sea circuit from Ashdod takes 8–10 hours. The circuit: Ashdod → Masada (1.5 hrs south-east) → cable car up + visit (2 hrs) → drive to Ein Bokek Dead Sea beach (30 min) → float + lunch (2 hrs) → return to Ashdod (2 hrs). This works for ships docked by 7 am with an 8 pm departure. For tighter schedules, choose one site over the other.

## Tel Aviv from Ashdod — the independent option

Tel Aviv is the easiest independent destination from Ashdod. At 35 km and 30–35 minutes by taxi, it requires no guide and no pre-arrangement beyond the taxi itself.

**Half-day Ashdod → Tel Aviv itinerary:**

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 9:00 am | Taxi from port to Jaffa Old City (~₪100–130) |
| 9:15–11:00 am | Jaffa: Clock Tower, port, flea market (Shuk HaPishpeshim), St Peter's Church views |
| 11:00 am–12:30 pm | Walk the Promenade (Tayelet) north — 4 km along the Mediterranean beachfront |
| 12:30–1:30 pm | Lunch near Carmel Market or Sarona Market |
| 1:30–2:30 pm | White City Bauhaus walk: Rothschild Boulevard |
| 3:00 pm | Taxi back to Ashdod (~₪110–140) |

**Getting around Tel Aviv independently:**
- Taxis queue throughout the city; Gett app works well in TLV
- Bird and Lime e-scooters are rentable by the minute along the promenade
- No Hebrew needed — English is widely understood in TLV

## Bethlehem from Ashdod

Bethlehem — home of the Church of the Nativity, one of Christianity's most sacred sites — lies about 1.5 hours from Ashdod and can be reached in a cruise day.

**Important access note:** Bethlehem is in the Palestinian Authority (Area A). Israeli rental cars are not permitted to cross into Area A. To visit Bethlehem independently, you need:
- A licensed Palestinian tour guide (required for Area A by most insurance policies and tour operators)
- Travel insurance that covers the West Bank
- Awareness that Checkpoint 300 (the main pedestrian crossing from Jerusalem) can have queues of 15–45 minutes on busy days

For cruise passengers, the cleanest option is a pre-booked excursion that combines Bethlehem with Jerusalem Old City in a single guided full-day itinerary. Many operators run this circuit from Ashdod and manage all border logistics.

## Port logistics: what to know

**Arriving at Ashdod port:**
- Cruise ships berth at Ashdod's dedicated cruise terminal
- Taxis queue at the port exit; negotiate a fixed price before getting in
- Port WiFi is limited — download offline maps (Maps.me or Google Maps offline) before docking
- The port terminal has a small café, duty-free and ATM

**Israeli entry:**
- All cruise passengers must clear Israeli immigration on arrival — this is typically done aboard ship; ship staff will advise on the process
- If you haven't applied for your ETA-IL (₪25, required for most nationalities, apply online at least 72 hours ahead), check with your cruise line — some handle this in advance

**Returning on time:**
- Build a 2-hour buffer before your ship's departure
- Have the ship's port agent phone number saved on your phone
- Pre-arrange return transport (don't rely on hailing taxis in Jerusalem's Old City during peak hours)
